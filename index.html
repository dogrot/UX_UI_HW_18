About Me

I'm a freelance UX/UI designer working from Minnesota in the United States.

Freshly graduated from the University of Minnesota’s online UX/UI boot camp. The class gave me a few projects under my belt and a good understanding of this field.

I strive to approach challenges from perspectives others wouldn’t think of, and often come up with more useful results because of this. I like to gather as much data as possible from as many different groups as I can, especially from groups that others might not consider asking, such as individuals with various disabilities and how they interact with the world.

My Story

Ever since I was a small child, I've enjoyed making art. No matter where life has taken me, I've always had this outlet for myself. I've never wanted to be a professional illustrator, I feared that doing so would make my hobby feel more like a job, and risk losing my passion for drawing.

When I discovered the field of UX/UI, it felt like the perfect balance of art and technology. I could use my artistic knowledge and skills in a way that made websites and apps into interfaces that looked pleasing to the eye and worked best for users.

I'm still relatively new to this field, but my passion for this new path of mine far outweighs any fears I may have. I look forward to creating more products and gaining more experience to shape myself into an even better and more confident designer.

“To me, UX/UI is a form of art, like painting or sculpting.”

- Quinn Watson

My UX Skills

User Research

I like to try and gather as much data as possible and search for it by asking individuals that might not be considered for interviewing. I try and cover all my bases and then some and often find my results are better because of that.

User Interface Design

UI design (especially visual design) is one of my favorite parts of UX/UI. I strive to create interfaces that are usable to as many people as possible and pleasing to use and look at. I’m particularly interested in accessibility UI and the aspects that surround it.

Web Development

I have a good understanding of how websites work behind the scenes. I’m more experienced with front-end development but am knowledgeable in back-end development and the commonly used tools of the trade for coding.
